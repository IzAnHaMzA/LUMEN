I. RATIONALE

As the automotive industry continues to embrace automation, electrification, and smart technologies, there is an increasing demand for skilled professionals capable of bridging traditional automotive engineering with modern electronics. These professionals must be equipped with the skills necessary to adapt to the rapidly evolving automotive landscape, where technology, electronics, and mechanics are becoming more integrated. This includes expertise in areas such as automotive sensors, actuators, control systems, and advanced vehicle diagnostics, all of which are crucial in shaping the future of the automotive industry.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to help the students to attain the following industry identified outcome through various teaching learning experiences: Perform remedial actions using advanced diagnostic tools of an automobile systems.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Apply the fundamentals of automotive digital electronics and Instrumentation.
  • CO2 - Interpret the Computer Electronic Control Module (ECM), different memories and vehicle network systems.
  • CO3 - Test the operation of automotive sensors and actuators.
  • CO4 - Evaluate the performance of vehicle control systems.
  • CO5 - Interpret diagnostic trouble codes in automotive systems.

 TLO 1.1 State the importance of digital electronics in automotive applications.

TLO 1.4 List the types of diodes used in automotive applications.

1.1 Introduction to Automotive Electronics.
1.2 Overview of Electrical Principles: Voltage, current, resistance, and power. Ohm’s Law and basic circuit analysis (Series and Parallel Circuits.)
1.3 Application of Zener Diode, Photo diode and LED in Automobiles.
1.4 Binary number system.
1.5 Instrumentation - Digital Cockpit, Heads Up Display.

TLO 2.4 Explain multiplexing improves communication in modern vehicle systems.

2.4 Multiplexing: - Principle of multiplexing.
