I. RATIONALE

This course explores advanced engine technologies that power modern vehicles. Students will gain comprehensive insights into the components of fuel supply systems and combustion process in petrol and diesel engines. Additionally, they acquire insights into digitally controlled engines, alternative energy sources and air pollution and emission control. This course integrates laboratory practice work focusing on component identification, inspection and maintenance of engine and other systems, to ensure optimal engine performance. This course is also prerequisite for the course ‘Vehicle Systems Maintenance’.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Maintain advanced automotive engines.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Detect abnormal combustion in I.C. engine.
  • CO2 - Use diagnostic equipment for effective engine fuel management.
  • CO3 - Apply the fundamental principles of advanced technology for better engine performance
  • CO4 - Perform I.C. engine test
  • CO5 - Apply basic concepts of emission control to reduce automotive pollution.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Select fuel for the given application/ engine for better performance.
TLO 1.2 Construct fuel supply system layout for gaseous fuel.
TLO 1.3 Describe the procedure to locate leakage in gaseous fuel supply system of the given type of engine.
TLO 1.4 Identify factors responsible for abnormal combustion in the given engine.
 Unit - I Engine Combustion and Alternative Fuels
1.1 Combustion in S.I. and C.I. Engines: Ignition limits, Air Fuel ratio in Petrol and Diesel engines, Stages of combustion in SI engine, I- head combustion chamber, Detonation and its control, Stages of combustion in CI engine, Diesel knock and its control, DI and IDI combustion chambers.
1.2 Properties of various fuels used in IC engines- Gasoline, Diesel, LPG, CNG, Hydrogen, Alcohol, Bio diesel.
1.3 Alternative Fuels for SI and CI engines- LPG, CNG, Alcohol, Bio- diesel (Merits and demerits with respect to performance and emission), CNG conversion kit block diagram. Leak detection procedure.

Presentations,
Model Demonstration,
Video Demonstrations.
2
 TLO 2.1 Explain function, location and working of component of given fuel supply system.
TLO 2.2 Describe the steps to scan the faults of the given MPFI engine.
TLO 2.3 Explain with sketch operation of the given component of CRDI system.
TLO 2.4 State manufacturer’s diagnostic procedure to diagnose the given fault in CRDI system using scan tools.
TLO 2.5 Interpret the given fault codes for the CRDI engine using scanner.
 Unit - II Engine Fuel Management
2.1 Fuel Management in S.I. engines: Throttle body injection (TBI) and Port fuel injection (PFI) systems; Methods of fuel Injection: Sequential, Continuous, grouped, simultaneous injection.
2.2 Sensors and Actuators of MPFI engine. Pressure regulators, fuel injector, and fuel pump.
2.3 Electronic Control Module: Input and output control functions as fuel injection, idle speed control, EGR, canister purge.
2.4 Diagnostic procedure of MPFI system: Use of Scan tool and reading fault codes .
2.5 Fuel Management in C.I. engines: Common Rail Direct Injection system: Major Components- Solenoid operated Fuel injector, High pressure fuel pump, High pressure accumulator.
2.6 CRDI System operation and advantages.
2.7 Diagnostic procedure of CRDI system; Use of Scan tool and reading fault codes.
2.8 Glow plugs: Construction, Circuit and operation of glow plug and its control unit.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Presentations,
Model Demonstration,
Video Demonstrations.

 TLO 3.1 Describe advanced features of the given turbocharger.
TLO 3.2 Select the engine with technology/ features suitable for the given application.
TLO 3.3 Compare the salient features of the given turbochargers.
TLO 3.4 List the salient features of the given injection system.
TLO 3.5 State the merits and demerits of using supercharger in S.I. engine
 Unit - III Advances in Automobile Engines
3.1 Turbocharger with wastegate boost control and its limitations .
3.2 Variable Geometric Turbocharger (VGT).
3.3 Variable Valve Timing mechanism (VVT).
3.4 Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) and Stratified-charge injection.
3.5 Supercharger.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Presentations,
Model Demonstration,
Video Demonstrations.

 TLO 4.1 Interpret the given engine performance parameters and their implications.
TLO 4.2 Explain with sketches the working principle of the given type of dynamometer.
TLO 4.3 Calculate engine Performance parameters for the given situation.
TLO 4.4 Prepare heat balance sheet for the given situation.
 Unit - IV Engine Performance
4.1 Engine Performance parameters. Definitions: Indicated Power, Brake Power and Frictional Power; Efficiencies - Air standard, Mechanical, Brake Thermal, Indicated Thermal, Volumetric and Relative.
4.2 Dynamometers and engine testing: Working Principle and types of Dynamometers: Hydraulic and Eddy current. Engine Testing: Morse Test, Willian’s line Method for finding Frictional Power. Heat balance sheet and Method of calculating Volumetric Efficiency and Fuel Consumption.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Presentations,
Model Demonstration,
Video Demonstrations.
5
 TLO 5.1 State the properties of the exhaust emission for the given engine.
TLO 5.2 Explain with sketch method of emission control for the given engine.
TLO 5.3 Tabulate prevailing Bharat Stage (BS) norms for given vehicle.
 Unit - V Fuel Economy and Emission control
5.1 Fuel economy and methods of improving fuel economy.
5.2 Petrol engine emission and its control: Pollutants from petrol engine. Engine design modification, treatment of exhaust gas- Catalytic Converter; fuel modification. Positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) system; Evaporation emission control system.
5.3 Diesel Engine emission and its control: Diesel engine pollutants, diesel smoke and control. Exhaust-Gas recirculation (EGR) – EGR valve and control system; Selective Catalytic Reduction.
5.4 Emission Norms for two, three and four-wheeler engines.
