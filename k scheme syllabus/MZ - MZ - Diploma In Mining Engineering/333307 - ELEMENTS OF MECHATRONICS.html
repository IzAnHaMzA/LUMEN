I. RATIONALE

The "Elements of Mechatronics" course equips diploma students in Mining with foundational knowledge of Electrical, Electronics, and Mechanical engineering, emphasizing their integration into mechatronic systems. This interdisciplinary approach enhances their ability to understand, modern mining equipment like conveyor systems, autonomous vehicles, and robotic arms. The curriculum bridges theoretical concepts with practical applications, fostering skills in automation, sensor interfacing, and system design, aligning with industry demands for technologically proficient mining professionals.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Develop the ability to integrate mechatronic systems for automated mining applications.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Use basic electrical laws for applications in mining-related systems.
  • CO2 - Identify electronics components in mining related systems.
  • CO3 - Identify mechanical components and systems in mining.
  • CO4 - Suggest use of IoT for mining applications.
  • CO5 - Suggest use of AI and robotics for mining applications.

 Unit - I Basics of Electrical Enginering
1.1 Basic definitions and Laws: Voltage, current, resistance, power, energy and their SI units. Ohm’s Law. Series-parallel circuit. Kirchhoff’s Laws.
1.2 Transformers: Principles, types, rating, e.mf. equation, losses in transformer and calculation of efficiency, applications of transformer in mining.
1.3 Circuit Protection Devices: Necessity, Fuses its types, Working of MCBs, and ELCBs and its applications.
1.4 Three-phase AC motors: Types, Squirrel cage Induction motor, working principle, connection diagram with DOL and star/delta starter, reversible direction and applications in mining.

 Unit - II Basics of Electronics
2.1 Introduction to Electronics: Conductors, semiconductors, and insulators. Definition of active and passive components. Passive components: Resistor, capacitor and inductor: symbol, types, working principle.
2.2 Active Components: Construction, working and applications of PN junction diode, PNP and NPN transistors.
2.3 Integrated Circuits: Definition and features: construction, and packaging, sizes, applications of ICs.
2.4 8051 Microcontroller: Block diagram, Pin diagram and its function and applications.
2.5 Sensors: Definition, working principle of Proximity, temperature, and pressure sensors and its applications in mining.

 Unit - III Basics of Mechanical Engineering
3.1 Fundamentals: Definitions of Force, torque, mechanical work, and power its SI units. Calculation of work and power.

3.3 Mechanical Actuators : Types of motion (Translational, Rotational), Gears: Gear trains, rack-and-pinion gears, Ratchet & Pawl, Bearings : Plain journal, Ball and roller bearings.
3.4 Electrical Actuators: Stepper motor – Types (Variable reluctance, Permanent magnet, Hybrid Stepper motor), Stepper motor specifications, Stepper motor Control.
