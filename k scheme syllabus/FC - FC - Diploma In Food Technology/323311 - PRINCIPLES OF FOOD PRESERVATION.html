I. RATIONALE

This course is essential in the food processing industry for applying suitable preservation methods for extending the shelf life of food. This course covers principles and methods involved in the preservation of food which mainly include high-temperature processing techniques like pasteurization, sterilization, and canning, as well as low-temperature methods such as chilling, freezing, drying, and dehydration. Additionally, the course explores the use of chemical preservatives and recent advancements like high-pressure processing (HPP), pulsed electric field (PEF), microwave-assisted thermal sterilization, ultrasound, and irradiation. This course will equip students with the skills required to extend the shelf life of food.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use suitable preservation method for extending shelf life of food.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Outline general principles of food preservation.
  • CO2 - Apply high temperature processing techniques for food preservation.
  • CO3 - Apply low temperature processing techniques for food preservation.
  • CO4 - Use chemical preservatives and drying methods for food preservation.
  • CO5 - Outline principle of preservation of recent methods of processing.
