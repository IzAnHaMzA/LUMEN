I. RATIONALE

This course focuses on identifying various sources of waste in the food industry, implementing effective waste management strategies, exploring opportunities for value-added product utilization, and ensuring compliance with environmental legislation and standards. This course knowledge will help to reduce food waste, optimize resource utilization, and adhere to environmental regulations. The course aims to foster sustainability and cost-effectiveness in the food industry.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to be help the student to attain the following industry-identified competency through various teaching-learning experience: Utilize to reduce environmental impact and operational costs in the food industry.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Enlist various sources of waste generated in food Industry.
  • CO2 - Utilize the food industry waste by using appropriate processes and preservation techniques.
  • CO3 - Apply the appropriate effluent treatment process in the food industry.
  • CO4 - Use solid waste treatment and disposal methods in food industry.
  • CO5 - Propose appropriate approach for waste treatment and management as per safety guidelines.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Describe the source and types of waste generated in food industry.
TLO 1.2 State the steps in waste management and food recovery hierarchy.
 Unit - I Introduction to Food Industry Waste.
1.1 Define Waste, Waste management, Types and Sources of Waste in the Food Industry, Waste Prevention Strategies, Waste Minimization Techniques, Waste Treatment Methods.
1.2 Impact of food waste- environmental, social, and economic, Factors contributes to Food waste, Waste management hierarchy, Food recovery hierarchy, Types of artificial intelligence in food waste management and its benefits.
Presentations
Lecture Using Chalk-Board,
Site/Industry Visit.
2
 TLO 2.1 Name the sources of waste and state its possible utilization of fruits and vegetables processing industry.
TLO 2.2 Describe the slaughterhouse and poultry processing waste nutitive value and its utilization.
TLO 2.3 Name the sources of carbohydrate and non-carbohydrate fraction waste and their possible utilization in cereal milling Industry.
TLO 2.4 Draw the oil seed processing and oil refining process flow sheet with waste output and possible utilization of them.
Unit - II Food Industry Waste and its Utilization.
2.1 Fruits and Vegetable processing industry - Waste generation in- Citrus fruit, Mango, Pineapple, Banana, Carrot and Tomato processing industry, Composition of waste and their possible utilization- Pectin, dietary fiber, biofuel , beverages and valorization
2.2 Slaughterhouse and Poultry processing industry –Waste generation in- Meat and Poultry processing industry, Composition of waste and their possible utilization-animal feed, biogas, compost and valorization, Solid Waste Treatment and Disposal Techniques.
2.3 Cereal Processing Industry- Waste generation in wheat, corn milling and brewing industry, Composition of waste- carbohydrate and non-carbohydrate fraction waste and their utilization- Animal Feed, Biogasl Production, Dietary fiber and protein isolates
2.4 Oil seed processing industry- Waste generation in oil seed processing and oil refining process, composition and its utilization.
 Presentations
Lecture Using Chalk-Board,
Site/Industry Visit,
Video Demonstrations.
3
 TLO 3.1 Define the basic properties of waste water.
TLO 3.2 Describe the Construction and working of preliminary treatment on liquid waste.
TLO 3.3 State the principle and process of primary and secondary treatment on waste water.
TLO 3.4 Draw the process flow sheet of liquid waste treatment used in food industry.
 Unit - III Effluent Treatment Plant.
3.1 Characteristics of waste water- Define the Hydraulic load, Organic Load, Raw waste load and waste from unit operations, Dissolve oxygen, Temperature, Oxygen demand (BOD and COD), suspended solids, Toxic compounds, pH, acidity and alkalinity, oil and grease and Salt.
3.2 Preliminary treatment- Construction and working of Screening, Sedimentation, Neutralization and oil and grease removal.
3.3 Primary and Secondary treatment -Construction and working of Sedimentation, Flotation, Anaerobic lagoons, Aerobic lagoons, Trickling filter, Activated sludge and Oxidation ditch
3.4 Tertiary treatment and Disposal- Process of chemical treatment, Carbon filter, Ion exchange filter and membrane-filtration, Liquid waste Disposal methods.
 Video Demonstrations,
Presentations
Lecture Using Chalk-Board,
Site/Industry Visit.
4
 TLO 4.1 Describe the nature and sources and types of solid waste in food industry.
TLO 4.2 Explain the different methods of solid waste disposal methods used in food industry.
TLO 4.3 State the possible utilization of solid waste in food industry.
TLO 4.4 Explain different methods used for solid waste disposal in food industry.
 Unit - IV Solid Waste Management.
4.1 Food solid Waste-Associate risks of solid waste, Source and Nature of solids in food Industry. Categories of solid waste, Primary and Secondary treatment on sludge,Types of sludge, sludge handling and sludge digestion.
4.2 Solid Waste Treatments- Sludge Thickeners, Dewatering Process-Vacuum filter and centrifuge, drying.
4.3 Solid waste utilization –Animal feed, Compost, Fuel, Preparation of waste oil and protein concentrate.
4.4 Solid waste Disposal-Composting, recycling, Landfill and Spread on land.
 Video Demonstrations
Lecture Using Chalk-Board,
Presentations,
Site/Industry Visit.
5
 TLO 5.1 Describe the features of state, national and international level environmental legislation bodies.
TLO 5.2 Describe the function and duties of the Central pollution control board.
TLO 5.3 Enlist name of documents requirement and process of apply pollution control certification.
TLO 5.4 State the effluent waste standards for Food processing Industries.
 Unit - V Environmental legislation.
5.1 ISO14000 EMS Policy overview, benefits, elements, general requirements, Procedure, six key elements and Importance of ISO-14001, Noise limit for generator setup run with diesel.
5.2 Central pollution control board- function, duties and objectives.
5.3 State pollution control board- functions, duties and objectives. Documents requirements, Procedure to apply certification and Industries categorization
5.4 Effluent waste standards for Food and Fruit processing Industry, Dairy and bakery Industry and Slaughter house.
 Presentations
Lecture Using Chalk-Board,
Video Demonstrations.
