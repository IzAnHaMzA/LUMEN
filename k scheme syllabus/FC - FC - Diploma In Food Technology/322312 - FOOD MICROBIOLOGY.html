I. RATIONALE

This course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of microorganisms in food technology. It covers the morphological and physiological characteristics of yeast, molds, and bacteria, media preparation, the use of various laboratory equipment, and plating techniques that are essential for microbial analysis of food. Additionally, it focuses on the causes and characteristics of food spoilage and foodborne illnesses, which are critical for ensuring food safety. This course will equip students with the skills needed to ensure food safety, prevent spoilage, and contribute to public health.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Analyze food for microorganisms responsible for food spoilages and borne illnesses.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Outline morphological & physiological characteristics of yeast, molds, and bacteria.
  • CO2 - Use various lab equipment’s and prepare media required for microbial analysis of food.
  • CO3 - Estimate microbial count using plating techniques.
  • CO4 - Analyze the causes and characterize spoilages in various food products.
  • CO5 - Analyze the causes and characterize various food born illness.
