I. RATIONALE

The course covers the basic engineering principles based on fundamentals of Thermodynamics, Fluid flow, Heat transfer, Refrigeration, Psychometric, refrigerated storage, and freeze-drying technology of food to applications in food process engineering. This knowledge shall be used for measuring and controlling the process parameters, designing and maintaining equipment, and developing food processing and storage lines.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
• Apply the principles of thermodynamics, fluid flow, heat transfer, refrigeration, and psychometry for food processing and preservation.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Define basic thermodynamic properties.
  • CO2 - Measure the basic properties of fluid flow.
  • CO3 - Apply the principle of heat transfer in heat processing of food.
  • CO4 - Illustrate the fundamental principles and applications of refrigeration and psychrometry.
  • CO5 - Use the refrigeration principle for food processing and storage.
