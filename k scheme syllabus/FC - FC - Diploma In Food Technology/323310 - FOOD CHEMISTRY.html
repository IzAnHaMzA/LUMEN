I. RATIONALE

Foods are complex substances composed of different chemical constituents. These constituents mainly include water, carbohydrates, proteins, lipids minerals, and vitamins. In addition, foods also contain small amounts of pigments, flavoring substances crude fibers. These constituents give foods their structure, texture, color, flavor, and nutritional value. Thus, this knowledge shall be used to control chemical changes that occur during the processing of foods.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Apply principles of food chemistry to control chemical changes occur during processing of foods.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Outline relationship between water activity and reaction rates in food.
  • CO2 - Classify the carbohydrate and Chemical reaction involving carbohydrates.
  • CO3 - List the sources of proteins and commercial uses of proteins.
  • CO4 - State the principles and significances of oil and fat, Mechanism of rancidity and its prevention..
  • CO5 - Outline Structure of pigments and occurrences of pigments
