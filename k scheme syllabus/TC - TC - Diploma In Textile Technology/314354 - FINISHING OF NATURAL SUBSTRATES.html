I. RATIONALE

Enhancing natural fibers through finishing is pivotal in elevating the market value of textiles. Proficiency in this area is essential for diploma engineers, equipping them to produce high-quality textiles tailored to specific end uses. This comprehensive course imparts vital concepts, skills, and techniques necessary for achieving superior finishing, enabling engineers to adeptly address diverse challenges in textile finishing.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Undertake finishing processes for the given natural fibre for end use.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Use relevant finishing methods on the given natural fabric.
  • CO2 - Select suitable softening and stiffening agents for the given natural fabric.
  • CO3 - Select suitable resin finish for the given natural fabric.
  • CO4 - Select suitable flame retardant for the given natural fabric.
  • CO5 - Select suitable antimicrobial finish on the given natural fabric.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Visit industries, market shops to collect at least 20 fabric samples of various dimensions. Find the current price and end use of these collected samples, present the results.
  • Collect different unfinished and finished samples and check the difference with respect to finishing, faults. Present the results.
  • Visit nearby industries and collect various softener and stiffener samples, test them in the lab and present their results.
  • Collect samples of resins from industries and make the comparative chart of their properties and applications.
  • Collect herbal finishes from the industries and see their effects on cotton fabric.
  • Visit local industry. Identify stenter, sanforiser and calender machines used in the industry. Prepare a machinewise chart incorporating various machine parts and their diagrams.
