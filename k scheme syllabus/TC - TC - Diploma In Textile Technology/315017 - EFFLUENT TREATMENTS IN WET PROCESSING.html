I. RATIONALE

The textile industry, one among the largest employer, forms a crucial part of the Indian economy. However, it significantly contributes to water pollution due to the chemicals used in wet processing. Stringent regulations govern the quality of wastewater released into natural water bodies. Therefore, students need to learn about sustainable technologies to mitigate these ecological issues. This course aims to equip diploma students with knowledge and skills of eco-friendly textile processing methods, enabling them to address the environmental challenges posed by the textile industry effectively.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
"Use sustainable technologies to mitigate ecological issues related to the pollution in textile processing"
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify toxic chemicals and dyes used in the textile industry.
  • CO2 - Choose the relevant testing method for testing textile effluent
  • CO3 - Select relevant physical and chemical methods for treating textile effluent.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Determine the effect of effluents received from different departments of a cotton processing plant on the germination of mono and dicotyledonous seeds and present the findings of the same.
  • Prepare an exhaustive matrix of different chemicals used in the treatment of textile effluents and present the findings of the same.
  • Compare the BOD and COD values of effluents received from different departments of a cotton processing plant and present the findings of the same.
  • Evaluate the effect of different coagulants and optimize the same for treatments of textile effluents and present the findings of the same.
  • Prepare an exhaustive matrix of different electrode combinations used in the electrolytic treatment of textile effluents along with the features of combinations selected and present the findings of the same.
  • Evaluate the effluent characteristics properties received from different departments of a cotton / polyester / PC blend processing unit and present the findings of the same.
    • Assignment
  • Prepare a detailed report of visited textile wet processing effluent plant.
  • Elaborate on the chemicals and their functions which are used for treating textile wet processing wastewaters.
  • Elaborate the Reverse Osmosis and Multiple Evaporation Techniques employed in the effluent treatment process.
  • Enlist and elaborate on the advantages and limitations of effluent treatment method used in common ETP .
