I. RATIONALE

Within the textile industry, diverse chemical processes are applied to various natural fibres. This process involves a profound understanding of the chemical compositions and morphological structures of these fibres, as well as their properties and ultimate applications. Diploma engineers are required to assimilate this knowledge and employ a pertinent methodology derived from a solid foundation in fibre science. This course is designed to equip learners with essential insights into natural fibres, empowering them to solve broad-based problems in the textile industry.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use relevant wet processing sequences according to the properties of various natural fibres.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select relevant natural fibres according to their end uses
  • CO2 - Test properties of cotton to suite relevant wet processing sequence
  • CO3 - Test properties of bast fibre to suite relevant wet processing sequence
  • CO4 - Test properties of leaf fibre to suite relevant wet processing sequence
  • CO5 - Test properties of wool fibre to suite relevant wet processing sequenc
  • CO6 - Test properties of silk fibre to suite relevant wet processing sequence.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Cotton Single Fibre Strength: Collect 10 different varieties of cotton fibre samples and measure their fineness using the cut weight method and compare it with the airflow principle instrument.
  • Wool fibre Length: Collect 10 different varieties of wool fibres, measure their lengths using the grease plate method and present the results.
  • Silk Fibre Damage: Collect 3 different silk fibre fabric samples. Carry out degumming and bleaching of silk fabric samples, assess them for damage and present the results.
  • Moisture Regain: Collect 10 different fibre samples and measure their moisture regain and moisture content using the relevant methods and present the results.
  • Collection of banana stem, extraction of fibres and making unconventional product (any 4) from the fibre.
    • Assignment
  • Prepare a chart showing morphological properties and L.S & T.S. of natural fibres
  • Prepare a chart showing physical, chemical properties and applications of natural fibres.
  • Prepare a pictorial presentation showing the sericulture of silk fibre.
