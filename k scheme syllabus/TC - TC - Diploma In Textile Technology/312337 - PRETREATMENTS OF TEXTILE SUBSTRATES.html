I. RATIONALE

Textile wet processing plays a crucial role in elevating the aesthetic and market value of fabrics. The achievement of these values is contingent upon successive dyeing, printing, and finishing treatments. For optimal outcomes in these processes, it is imperative that the fabric is free from both added and acquired impurities, such as size, oil, fat, wax, and colour. Therefore, the removal of these impurities becomes indispensable to ensure the effectiveness of dyeing, printing, and finishing processes. This course is designed to equip diploma engineers with comprehensive knowledge of textile pre-treatment technologies. The primary objective is to empower them to render fabrics free from added and acquired impurities. By delving into the intricacies of textile substrate pre-treatment, learners will gain invaluable insights into the methods and techniques essential for preparing fabrics for subsequent processing stages.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use relevant textile pretreatment technologies for the given textile substrate.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select the relevant mechanical process to remove the impurities from textiles.
  • CO2 - Use the relevant desizing process to remove size from the textile.
  • CO3 - Use the relevant scouring process to remove hydrophobic impurities from the textile.
  • CO4 - Use the relevant bleaching process to improve the whiteness of the textile.
  • CO5 - Select the relevant mercerization process to improve the luster of cotton.
