I. RATIONALE

In textile industry, the quality textile is manufactured through many processes such as dyeing, printing, and finishing. These major processes improve the aesthetic as well as the market value of the textile. The knowledge and skills related to dyeing of natural fibres is essential for the diploma engineer to create quality textile. This course is developed in such a way that basic concepts and principles of dyeing of natural fibres and their application methods will help the diploma engineer to get quality dyed yarn and fabrics and solve broad based problems in the textile colouration processes.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
• Use relevant dyes, chemicals, dyeing equipment and dyeing processes for natural substrates.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select relevant pretreatment process and dyeing machines for dyeing of natural fibres.
  • CO2 - Use relevant dyeing method for the given cellulosic material with direct and reactive dyes.
  • CO3 - Use relevant dyeing method for the given cellulosic material with vat and sulphur dyes.
  • CO4 - Use relevant dyeing method for the given cellulosic material with azoics colours.
  • CO5 - Use relevant dyeing method for the given protein material with relevant dyes.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Identify dyeing faults: Visit nearby industries and collect dyed samples of faulty dyeing and find remedies to rectify the same. Present your report.
  • Lab to bulk dyeing: Collect any two cotton dyed samples from industry with their recipe and produce same results in laboratory on OBBD machine by changing appropriate recipe. Correlate the results and present.
  • Water consumption: Visit any textile dye house. Collect information of any two dyeing machines for their water consumption. Calculate the quantity of water consumption per kg of fabric. Present your report.
  • Shade matching: Collect any one dyed sample from nearby dye house. Using any class of dye match the shade in the laboratory. Present your matched samples with recipe.
  • Fastness properties: Collect any one dyed sample from the nearby dye house and assess its fastness to washing, rubbing and perspiration. Present your ratings.
    • Assignment
  • Calculate dye and chemical cost: Collect a dyeing recipe, cost of dyes and chemicals used for any two dyeing methods from a nearby process house and calculate dyeing cost. Prepare and submit the results.
  • Library/ internet survey: Identify developments in dyes, dyeing machines and dyeing process used for cellulosic dyeing. Prepare and submit the report.
  • Safety in process house: Collect information on safety precautions of various chemicals and machinery used in the process house. Prepare and submit the report.
