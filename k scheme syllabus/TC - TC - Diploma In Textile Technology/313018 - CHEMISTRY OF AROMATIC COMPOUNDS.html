I. RATIONALE

In the textile wet processing journey, dyeing and printing emerge as crucial stages, elevating both the visual appeal and market worth of fabrics. Synthetic dyes, primarily composed of aromatic organic compounds, play a key role in wet processing. This course imparts essential knowledge on the physical and chemical properties of aromatic compounds, aiding diploma engineers in grasping the intricacies of dye structure. By applying fundamental concepts of aromatic chemistry, diploma engineers will gain the skills to address diverse challenges in dyeing and printing processes, enhancing their problem-solving abilities.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
• Apply principles of color theory, chemical composition, structure, properties of dyes, dye intermediates and their synthesis in textile processing
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select relevant aromatic compounds for manufacturing of dye intermediates.
  • CO2 - Prepare relevant azo dyes by diazotization and coupling.
  • CO3 - Select relevant dyes for dyeing of fabrics
  • CO4 - Use relevant processes for testing dyes.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • Draw the structures of aromatic compounds with different functional groups and write their common names. List their applications in textile industry.
  • Draw the structures of any four types of dyes and identify the functional groups present in it.
  • Classify dyes based on functional groups present in dye molecules.
  • Collect structures of different class of the dyes and identify chromophore and auxochrome present in it.
    • Micro project
  • Collect polyester fabric samples dyed with different dyes and determine their sublimation fastness.
  • Find the structures of reactive dyes using Colour index database. Find their CI number and constitution number.
  • Use aniline derivatives for diazotization and find colour of the dye formed on cotton hank
  • Collect different dye samples from near by industry and check dispersing properties of dye using filter paper test.
