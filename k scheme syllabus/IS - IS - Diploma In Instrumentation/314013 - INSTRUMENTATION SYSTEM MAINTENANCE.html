I. RATIONALE

Maintenance of instrumentation systems ranging from simple gauges to complex distributed control systems is vital in any process industry. This course aims to develop various technical skills such as preventive maintenance, calibration and troubleshooting of instruments which will help the instrumentation diploma holder to effectively upkeep the instrument systems in the work place.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to help the student to attain the following industry /employer expected outcome through various teaching learning experiences: Maintain the instrumentation systems in process industries.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select appropriate maintenance and troubleshooting techniques for instrumentation systems.
  • CO2 - Maintain field instruments in a process industry.
  • CO3 - Maintain control loops implemented in a process industry.
  • CO4 - Calibrate measuring and control instruments installed in process plants.

1.1 Maintenance: need of instruments/control systems maintenance, types of maintenance - preventive, predictive, corrective maintenance

 Unit - II Preventive Maintenance and Troubleshooting of Field Instruments
2.1 Preventive maintenance (PM)- elements, role of instrument maintenance technician/engineer, PM plan and schedule, safety and health practices
2.2 Preventive maintenance checklists and troubleshooting guidelines - temperature, pressure, flow, level measuring instruments/transmitters, load cell, linear variable differential transformer (LVDT), pressure switch, level switch
2.3 Preventive maintenance checklists and troubleshooting guidelines - control valves, actuators, current to pressure (I/P) converter, pressure to current (P/I) converter, electro-pneumatic valve positioner, pressure regulator cum filter, alarm annunciator, indicators

 TLO 4.1 Identify appropriate calibration standards and equipment.
TLO 4.2 Describe the steps for calibration of temperature measuring instrument PT-100/thermocouple by fixed point cell/comparison method.
TLO 4.3 Describe the steps for calibration of pressure gauges using dead weight tester.
TLO 4.4 Describe the steps for calibration of pressure or temperature transmitters using multi-function calibrator.
TLO 4.5 Explain the procedure for calibration of smart transmitter using HART communicator.
TLO 4.6 Explain the procedure for calibration of control valve-actuator and its auxiliary components.
 Unit - IV Calibration of Measuring and Control Instruments
4.1 Calibration: need of calibration, types of calibration standards, traceability concept, field and bench calibration, individual instrument and loop calibration, National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratory (NABL) accredited certificate cum report
4.2 Temperature calibration: temperature standards, fixed point cell and comparison methods, standard temperature sources, calibration of temperature measuring instruments-bimetallic thermometer, PT100, thermocouples, pyrometers, temperature switches
4.3 Pressure calibration: pneumatic/hydraulic dead weight tester, pneumatic comparator with master pressure gauge, multi-function calibrator, pressure gauges, electronic and smart pressure transmitters, LVDT, strain gauge, load cell type pressure transmitter
4.4 Flow calibration: gravimetric, volumetric, master flowmeter methods, calibrating liquid/gas flowmeters - turbine, ultrasonic, mass flowmeters, positive-displacement flowmeter, rotameter
4.5 Calibration of level instruments: differential pressure cell type, capacitance type, ultrasonic type level transmitters
4.6 Calibration of control valves, actuators, current to pressure (I/P) converter, pressure to current (P/I) converter, electro-pneumatic valve positioner
