I. RATIONALE

Building automation system is essential to ensure smart building facilities such as lighting, heating, ventilation, air conditioning (HVAC), security, and energy management. The knowledge of building automation system is essential for instrumentation diploma students. This course is designed to familiarize the diploma students with various aspects of building automation system.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to help students to attain the following industry/employer expected outcome through various teaching learning experiences. "Maintain various sub systems of building automation system"
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Describe the various components of building automation system.
  • CO2 - Maintain the different BAS Subsystems.
  • CO3 - Maintain HVAC system and its components.
  • CO4 - Control various parameters of BAS using direct digital controllers.
  • CO5 - Use BAS software for effective facility control.

 Unit - III Heating ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) systems
3.1 Air properties - Dry bulb temperature, Wet bulb temperature, Relative humidity, Humidity ratio, Dew point temperature, Enthalpy, Specific volume (Definitions only).

3.5 Control strategies for occupant comfort - Control methods, Zone control, Temperature control, Humidity control. Control of primary equipment - Hot water system, Cold water system. Control of distribution system - Fan-volume and pressure control, Pumps- volume and pressure control. .
3.6 Maintenance of HVAC system.
