I. RATIONALE

The goal of the course is to increase students' skills and proficiency in Indian regional cuisine and street food, with focus on bulk operational procedures used in large scale food production. This will assist students in producing large quantities of food, thus facilitating the catering industry.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The objective of this course is to assists the students in gaining the following competencies through a variety of teaching and leaning experiences : To cook regional cuisines in bulk
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify the various equipment used in bulk cooking
  • CO2 - Practice bulk purchase procedure and use various commodities in quantity cookery
  • CO3 - Make indent for volume feeding
  • CO4 - Select regional masalas and gravies for cooking
  • CO5 - Prepare cuisine and menu for regional festival,staple diet and street food.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Visit a bulk cooking unit in a group of five students, and observe how each kitchen is laid (Layouts) up in terms of food preparation and service. Evaluate the design and provide the required fixes
  • Investigate the different festive foods from the region and how they are presented.
  • In a group of 5 students conduct a market analysis of the different regional Indian specialty restaurants in your city, looking at the menu from a regional perspective. Apply what you've learned to the decor and taste characteristics of the region. Write a report on the subject and deliver it.
  • Take a group of five students to your city's bulk cooking facilities, hotels, and restaurants to observe the manufacturers and contemporary equipment utilized in local bulk cooking
  • Go to several eateries and hotels and get banquet menus for Indian cuisine. Enumerate the dishes and evaluate them based on the following criteria. 1 How did the selection of the dishes come about? 2 Do the dishes go well together? 3 What other foods may have been recommended?
  • Visit a Railway catering unit and study the workflow and packing of food produced. Critic the layout with necessary solutions.
  • In a group of five students visit to a market, and note what you see about the different local vegetables that are on sale. Enumerate their regional names
  • Perform a skit on food waste to achieve sustainability development goals no 2 (Zero Hunger )
    • Assignment
  • Enlist 10 equipment according to functional classification.( Preparation cooking, Service )
  • Enlist 10 regional restaurant in and around your city
  • Enlist 10 bulk equipment manufactures and suppliers of each
  • List 20 Proprietary masala with their brand names in your city
  • List down 10 traditional street food of your city and their food presentation
