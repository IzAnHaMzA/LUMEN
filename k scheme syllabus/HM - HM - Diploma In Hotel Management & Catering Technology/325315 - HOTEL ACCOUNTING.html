I. RATIONALE

“Accounting is a salt and spice for the business”. Accounting is also a contemporary language of the business while book keeping is an art & technique. Explaining principles, procedure, using tools of accounting along with their advantages and limitations would be essential to cope up with the industry. Costing the activity and fixing selling price is a big task for hotel / hospitality industry, pricing too high may frightens the guest and too low may leads the business to losses. Taking right decisions accurately and timely is a good sign of healthy management.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Apply accounting principles, procedure, tools and practices to handle accounting functions of a hotel business identifying current market condition to ensure the overall growth and optimum profitability.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Prepare guest and supplier account in the books of hotel & Interpret Ledger balances.
  • CO2 - Write hotel transactions in Cash Book and reconcile with bank pass book.
  • CO3 - Generate Guest Weekly Bill using Visitors Tabular Ledger.
  • CO4 - Prepare hotel operating cost sheet and budget for the given activity.
  • CO5 - Select best suitable option to ensure optimum profitability for the hotel business.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Use double entry system.
TLO 1.2 Identify the scope of book-keeping in hotel business.
TLO 1.3 Prepare guest and supplier account in the books of hotel & Interpret Ledger balances.
 Unit - I Book-keeping
1.1 Transaction: Meaning and Classification of transaction as Real and Nominal, Mode of settlement as cash and personal (credit) with rules of debit and credit under double entry system.
1.2 Meaning and scope of book-keeping in hotel business . Conceptual study of Cash and accrual system as well as Single entry and Double entry system.
1.3 Ledger – meaning, format (Traditional and Modern), posting of transactions, balancing the ledger and interpretation of balances drawn.
Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Video Demonstrations
2
 TLO 2.1 Recognise the motive of holding cash and opening bank account.
TLO 2.2 Prepare Petty cash book.
TLO 2.3 Write Triple column Cash Book and reconcile with pass book.
 Unit - II Cash Book
2.1 Cash – meaning and motives of holding cash. Types of Bank account with features. Concept and types of Cheques, Demand Draft, Debit Cards, Credit Cards, Bank overdraft and Cash credits. Online Transfer (Net Banking, RTGS, NEFT, UPI).
2.2 Petty Cash Book: Meaning, Types and preparation of Analytical Petty Cash Book (under imprest system).
2.3 Cash book and types – Single, Double and Triple Column cash book. Preparation of triple column cash book to record hotel transactions and reconciliation with bank pass book.
Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Demonstration
3
 TLO 3.1 Prepare marginal and departmental income statement to show profit and percentage with sale.
TLO 3.2 Identify the need of up-to-date guest record.
TLO 3.3 Generate Guest weekly bill using data from visitors tabular ledger.
 Unit - III Hotel Records and Billing
3.1 Departmental and Marginal Income Statement: Concept, elements (Sale, Material consumed, Labour cost, Overheads), standard format, Preparations of marginal and departmental statements and show percentage analysis with sales.
3.2 Visitors Tabular Ledger (VTL): - Meaning, Features, Purpose and Format. (Note: As large data required to draft VTL, Class-room demonstration & Micro project on VTL is suggested).).
3.3 Guest Weekly Bill (GWB): Meaning, purpose, distinction between VTL and GWB, Format (Computerized and Manual). Generate Guest Weekly Bill.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Demonstration
4
 TLO 4.1 Recognise and classify cost.
TLO 4.2 Prepare hotel operating cost sheet.
TLO 4.3 Prepare Budget for various hotel activities.
 Unit - IV Hotel Costing and Budgeting
4.1 Cost & Costing – meaning, types with features, Cost centres, Cost units.
4.2 Classification of operating cost as Investment, Standing and Running. Calculation of effective room days and preparation of hotel operating cost sheet to fix the tariff.
4.3 Budget: Meaning, Classification, Merits, Preparation of Production Budget, Purchase Budget and Flexible budget for the given activity levels.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Demonstrations
5
 TLO 5.1 Recognize the role of BEP in hotel business.
TLO 5.2 Calculate BEP and allied values.
TLO 5.3 Select best suitable option for optimum profitability.
Unit - V Break Even Point
5.1 Concept of BEP (break even point) with the help of graph plotting Fixed & Variable cost, Sales, BEP, Margin of safety (MOS). Role of Profit Volume ratio (P/V Ratio).
5.2 Calculation of P/V ratio, BEP, MOS, Expected sales to make desired profit, Expected profit on desired sale.
5.3 Decision making for optimum profitability – Make or Buy, Drop or Select product or product line, Accept or reject special price offer, New product mix under key factor.
Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Demonstration

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Plan and Estimate cost of Menu (Starters, Beverages, Main course & Desserts) for event applying the knowledge of Sales mix, yield and single costing.
  • Estimate the Room Rent for a hotel using Service or operating costing.
  • Estimate the itinerary for 5 days and 4 nights Tour package (Ticketing, Stay, Food, Entry Fee, Local Transport, Guide service, Tips all inclusive) using service/operating costing.
  • Calculate Break-even point in quantity and in rupees for opening a new restaurant.
  • Prepare Production budget and purchase budget for Freshers Party, the event for 250 pax.
  • Visit a hotel, collect the data & Prepare Visitors Tabular ledger (VTL) for consecutive four days and prepare Guest weekly bill (GWB) of specific room.
  • Collect required data from nearby daily needs shop or bakery shop to pass entries for GST and Input Tax Credit (ITC).
  • Collect data from nearby hotel and prepare Cash Book with Cash and Bank columns.
  • Collect data from office establishment and prepare analytical petty cash book
  • Prepare tour operating cost sheet taking itinerary to fix the selling price for your relatives. At least Two.
  • Collect data from any banquet hall and prepare a flexible budget for various capacities (from 0 to 100 % activity levels)
  • Collect the data from cash book from nearby hotel or restaurant and reconcile with bank pass book

  • Collect set of transaction from nearby hotel and classify as Real, Nominal and Personal
  • Collect the the cost data from nearby hotel and classify.
  • Draft standard format of cash book and analytical petty cash book.
  • Draft standard format of visitors tabular ledger (VTL).
  • Collect number of rooms and seasonal impact data from nearby hotels and calculate absolute room days and effective room days
  • Visit nearby hotels and identify the types of budgets prepared by the hotel.
  • Prepare chart to display various types of cheques using colour images.
  • Prepare one chart to illustrate the process of balancing the ledger jointly by the class and display in class room.
  • Prepare a chart showing rules for recording transaction and mode of settlement as Debit (Dr.) and Credit (Cr.) and paste in front of your study table.
  • Identify, mark and prepare a list of cost centres from nearby hotels
  • Visit at least 10 hotels and collect the samples of guest weekly bill. (with cancelled or duplicate stamp)
