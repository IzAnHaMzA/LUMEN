I. RATIONALE

Food and Beverage system is crucial for ensuring that restaurant sales cover all expenses and generate a reasonable profit. While the system itself doesn't solve problems, its effectiveness depends on proper policies and operational procedures. Management must actively review the data and make informed decisions to improve performance. This course will develop skills in using the procedures involved in revenue control. The skills developed in this course will enable the students to get placements as professional bartenders.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The theory, practical experience and relevant soft skills associated with this course are to be taught and implemented, so that the students demonstrate the following industry-oriented COs associated with the above-mentioned competency: Control the expense and maximize the revenue generation in food and beverages to earn more profit.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Serve the dishes using gueridon trolley
  • CO2 - Execute the duties and responsibilities of bar staff
  • CO3 - Implement food and beverage controls
  • CO4 - Maintain and improve purchase procedures in a hotel industry
  • CO5 - Follow the revenue control procedures in the hotels

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Prepare and submit a standard operating procedure (SOP) document for gueridon service, including setup, execution, and closing
  • Record a short video (3–5 minutes) demonstrating the mise-en- place required for a gueridon service for a specific dish
  • Research and present a visual guide on 8 types of bars, Include pictures, key features, and target customer segments
  • Prepare chart for mixed drinks with suggesting correct glassware with colour codes
  • Design a professional beverage list for a theme-based bar (e.g., vintage bar, tropical tiki bar)
  • Plan a mini cocktail/mocktail event with a specific theme (e.g., "Tropical Paradise" or "Hollywood Classics"). Create a drink menu, bar setup plan, and guest service strategy
  • Prepare a presentation on effective storage and issuing techniques, covering aspects like temperature control, inventory rotation, and loss prevention
  • Develop a policy manual outlining the objectives and fundamentals of food & beverage control in a restaurant or hotel, include cost control measures, wastage reduction techniques, and common obstacles
  • Design a Banquet Bar System setup plan for an event, cover bar layout, staffing, inventory checklist, and service procedures
  • Propose a pricing strategy for a bar/restaurant to increase beverage profitability while maintaining customer satisfaction, Include cost analysis, price elasticity, and promotional strategies
    • Assignment
  • Design a poster explaining the safety precautions for flambé dishes
  • Develop strategy for macerated drinks by infusing mixed drinks with herbs and spices in correct combination for enhanced flavors (R&D)
  • Design a sample beverage menu with calculated cost per drink and selling price
  • Create a flowchart illustrating the food and beverage purchasing process, from order planning to supplier selection and delivery, prepare purchase specifications for key ingredients and beverages
  • Identify and analyze common hurdles in beverage control (e.g., spillage, theft, incorrect pouring), suggest solutions and preventive measures for each challenge
    • Activities
  • Search in the search engines and design proposal for an ideal gueridon trolley, considering factors like storage, mobility, and aesthetics
  • Develop transperant wax jelly models for glassware depicting the replica of the famous cocktails
  • Make a short video to Show the difference between overpouring and standard pouring and its impact on costs
  • Research and list at least five proven techniques that restaurants and bars use to increase beverage revenue, provide real-world examples and analyze their effectiveness
  • Make a special promotional beverage display board in a bar
