I. RATIONALE

Water has always been vital for agriculture, from the ancient irrigation systems of early civilizations to today's advanced technologies. This course explores the evolution of irrigation and drainage systems, from past techniques like channels and aqueducts to modern methods such as drip and sprinkler systems with automated controls. Students will learn how to implement efficient, cost-effective water management practices that enhance productivity and sustainability. Looking ahead, the course also introduces innovations like smart irrigation networks that use real-time data, ensuring agriculture remains resilient amid global challenges like water scarcity and environmental change.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Design irrigation and drainage systems for productivity, cost efficiency, environmental impact, and overall risk management.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select suitable methods for measuring irrigation water.
  • CO2 - Use the soil-water-plant relationship for irrigation scheduling.
  • CO3 - Identify irrigation methods according to crop.
  • CO4 - Suggest the micro irrigation system for efficient utilization of water.
  • CO5 - Use appropriate surface and subsurface drainage systems for given field.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • Prepare a list of crops suitable for border irrigation.
  • Study of minimum two types of Control and Automation in Micro-irrigation such as Open loop and Closed loop control, Volume-Based Automated Irrigation Systems, Time-Based Automated Irrigation Systems, Sequential and Non-Sequential Automated Irrigation Systems, Feedback control systems, etc.
  • Identify water sources and prepare data on water availability of your college campus/village.
  • Suggest irrigation methods for your college farm/village.
  • Prepare a list of crops suitable for furrow irrigation.
  • Identify waterlogged areas of your college campus/village.
  • Study the various types of flumes and orifices used in flow measurement.
  • Draw and study soil moisture characteristics curve.
  • Draw a chart of irrigation methods.
  • Identify salt-affected soil and suggest appropriate reclamation measures.
  • Collect information about various irrigation schemes and grant-in-aid assistance from the Government.
    • Micro project
  • Collect climatological data of any three quantities such as temperature, rainfall, wind velocity, and humidity from nearby observatories to calculate water requirements for specified areas.
  • Prepare a detailed report on the financial assistance of central / state Government schemes for the Jalayukt shivarYojana / Farm Ponds / Drip Irrigation scheme.
  • Classify any two seasonal crops and find out the average water requirement of selected crops.
  • Create a detailed pictorial chart of drip irrigation components, including specifications such as pressure, material cost, etc.
  • Survey of water sources in the specified area, including a minimum of two samples from each type of source (river, well, tap, bore well) to check the various chemical properties of water.
  • Conduct a field survey of diverse soil types within a specified region and then prepare and submit a comprehensive report on soil classification.
  • Determine soil moisture content, field capacity, and wilting point constants for a minimum of two soil samples from the field.
  • Give step-by-step design procedures for border and check basin irrigation methods.
  • Evaluate the effects of surface irrigation versus micro irrigation on crop yield and water conservation in a specific region. Determine which method is more sustainable and explain why.
  • Prepare a model of a drip irrigation system.
  • Prepare a model of contour farming.
  • Prepare a chart showing the drainage systems of the field.
