I. RATIONALE

Organic farming have inclusion of natural resources where farmers use naturally available organic inputs like as crop residues, farm and animal waste, farm yard manure, compost, vermi-compost, green manure, bio-fungicides, bio-pesticides, bio-fertilizer, bio-agents to grow crops and raise animals. It is a sustainable and environmentally friendly way of farming that aims to minimize the use of synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and other harmful chemicals. Organic farming practices also promote soil health, biodiversity, and animal welfare.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Apply the latest techniques for organic crop production through the use of organic inputs like manure, compost, organic fertilizer, and pesticides for developing biodiversity, improving soil health, and ensuring food safety.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Develop Public awareness about organic farming.
  • CO2 - Recommend the process for organic farming to reduce soil degradation.
  • CO3 - Choose methods to control disease and pest in organic farming
  • CO4 - Use national standard norms to manage organic farm.
  • CO5 - Identify minimum requirement for certification and accreditation for organic farming.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • Prepare a report on APEDA organic food exported data from last five years.
  • Collect the information of a minimum 5 crops mostly grown in organic farming and prepare presentation.
  • Prepare a report format for certification and accreditation.
  • Collect the information about how to make seed balls.
  • Prepare presentation on step by step procedure for making jiv-amrut.
  • Investigate natural pest control methods such as companion planting, insect-repelling plants, and homemade organic pesticides.
  • Collect information about minimum two preventive methods used for weed management in organic farm. and prepare presentation.
  • Collect soil samples from a minimum two different locations and analyze their physical and chemical properties.
    • Micro project
  • Arrange Seed Saving Program: making seed ball for fruits seeds.
  • Start Community Garden Initiative: Start a community garden where residents can come together to grow organic fruits, vegetables, and herbs. This promotes community engagement while encouraging organic farming practices.
  • Organize Composting Workshop: Organize a workshop to teach local communities how to compost kitchen waste and organic materials to create nutrient-rich compost for their gardens or farms.
  • Organize Organic Pest Control Workshops at college
  • Collect Soil Health card from 10 local farmers and make report.
  • Arrange tours of local organic farms to educate the public about organic farming practices.
  • Urban Rooftop Gardens: Collaborate with building owners or community organizations to establish organic rooftop gardens in urban areas.
  • Prepare street play on organic farming awareness.
  • Develop and set up a composting system using organic waste material such as kitchen scraps, yard trimming and manure.
