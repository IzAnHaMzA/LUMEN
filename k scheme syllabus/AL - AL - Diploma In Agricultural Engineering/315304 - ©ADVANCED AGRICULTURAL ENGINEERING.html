I. RATIONALE

Advanced Agricultural Engineering is designed to equip diploma-level students with foundational and practical knowledge in modern agricultural technologies. It introduces essential concepts such as remote sensing, GIS, drones, vertical farming, protected cultivation, and integrating IoT and smartphones in agriculture. By simplifying complex topics and emphasizing real-world applications, the syllabus aims to prepare students for the challenges of contemporary farming, promoting sustainable practices and efficient resource management. This course bridges traditional agricultural wisdom with modern innovations, fostering a holistic approach to agricultural engineering.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use modern agricultural technologies with sustainable practices to solve agricultural problems.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Understand the principles and technologies of agriculture processes.
  • CO2 - Apply various tools for the given agricultural problem.
  • CO3 - Analyze the applications of drones in agriculture.
  • CO4 - Evaluate protected cultivation techniques for given agricultural situation.
  • CO5 - Use the concepts of vertical farming in given situation.

Micro project

  • Collect any one drone’s five parts information and its functions.
  • Make a model of Vertical Farming and show its various components.
  • Make any one model of Greenhouse or Polyhouse.
  • Make an information report on the ‘E-Peek-Pahani’ app of the Maharashtra State Agriculture Department.
  • Collect and study a minimum of three Journal Papers on IoT in Agriculture.
  • Prepare a two or three-page document on the historical evolution of drones and their technological advancements in agricultural use.
  • Compile a comparative study on various types of vertical farming systems concerning a minimum of two crops.
  • Prepare one small-scale model of a hydroponic system.
  • Identify and analyze the properties of traditional and innovative materials used for constructing farm animal shelters.
