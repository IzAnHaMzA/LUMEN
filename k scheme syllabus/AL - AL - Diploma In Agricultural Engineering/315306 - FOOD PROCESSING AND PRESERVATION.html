I. RATIONALE

Food preservation and processing are crucial aspects of agricultural engineering, focusing on maintaining food quality, safety, and nutritional value from farm to table. This field involves applying various techniques such as drying, freezing, packaging, and storage to prevent spoilage, extend shelf life, and ensure food availability throughout the year. By mastering these techniques, students can contribute to reducing food waste, enhancing food security, and supporting sustainable agricultural practices.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Recognize food spoilage causes, recommend preservation methods, and utilize processing techniques to enhance the shelf life of agricultural products.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify the key concepts of food preservation.
  • CO2 - Suggest the food processing techniques for different agricultural products.
  • CO3 - Illustrate the various preservation Techniques.
  • CO4 - Identify the food additives and their role in food processing.
  • CO5 - Analyze food process engineering related operations.
