I. RATIONALE

The course covers essential processing techniques that agricultural materials undergo from harvest to becoming a final consumer product. It introduces students to past, present, and future processing operations such as cleaning, grading, drying, milling, and thermal processing of various commodities. By the end of the course, students will understand the principles and operations of processing machinery, enabling them to set up their own processing units. Additionally, the practical skills gained will be invaluable for completing future projects in post-harvest processing.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
This course aims to help the student attain the following industry/employer expected outcome through various teaching-learning experiences: Suggest appropriate operations performed on agricultural produce after harvesting.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify the engineering properties of given agricultural produce.
  • CO2 - Apply cleaning and grading techniques for agricultural produce.
  • CO3 - Analyze various storage methods for the given environmental condition.
  • CO4 - Evaluate practical skills and knowledge for effective unit operations of farm produce.
  • CO5 - Use various agricultural processing techniques to enhance the quality of agricultural produce.
