I. RATIONALE

Historically, seed processing was crucial for seed quality and viability, relying on manual labor and basic machinery. With advancements in agricultural practices, the field evolved to incorporate more sophisticated techniques. Recent innovations have markedly enhanced the precision and effectiveness of seed processing, with a 30% increase in processing efficiency and a 40% improvement in seed quality. Modern seed process engineering now leverages advanced machinery and techniques, integrating expertise in state-of-the-art equipment, and precise treatment methods, while addressing sustainability and environmental concerns. This course provides students with the skills to operate advanced equipment, implement effective treatments, and manage seed testing, handling, storage, certification, and marketing. Future trends indicate a shift towards AI and machine learning for enhanced precision and eco-friendly practices. The course prepares students to lead and innovate in these emerging areas, positioning them to drive the future of seed process engineering

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
This course aims to help the student attain the following industry/employer expected outcome through various teaching-learning experiences: Demonstrate expertise in operating, and managing seed processing systems.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Understand the principle of underlying seed processing.
  • CO2 - Illustrate the operation of seed processing equipment for a given seed sample.
  • CO3 - Apply appropriate seed treatment methods for a given seed samples.
  • CO4 - Recommend and evaluate methods for testing quality of a given seed samples.
  • CO5 - Prescribe the seed certification process, effective handling, seed packaging and storage techniques for a seed sample.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • Prepare a report on conventional seed processing practices.
  • Prepare a report on equipment required for single seed lot processing as well as multiple seed lot processing.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams of minimum three seed cleaning or grading equipment.
  • Draw neat, labelled diagrams of various materials handling equipment used in seed processing plant/industry.
  • Give criteria of site selection for seed testing laboratory.
  • Discuss the role of seed certification in the overall quality assurance of seeds.
  • How does certification impact seed production, processing, and distribution?
  • Identify common challenges faced in the seed certification process.
  • Compare given seed storage techniques to determine their effectiveness in maintaining seed viability over time.
  • Explore the use of digital marketing tools and platforms to promote seed products.
    • Micro project
  • Prepare charts of minimum three seed processing equipment.
  • Create seed bank of minimum five traditional seeds which are grown in your region.
  • Prepare flowchart showing all unit operations involved in seed processing.
  • Identify raw materials required for building the prototype for given seed processing equipment (e.g., screens with different mesh sizes, motors, fans, and containers).
  • Conduct a case study on the seed certification process for any two seeds.
  • Visit any three nearby farmer service centres and collect the information on different varieties of any five types of crop seeds which are grown in your region.
  • Prepare report on seed marketing by visiting sales persons of any two seed processing industry.
  • Investigate alternative eco-friendly materials for seed packaging.
  • Evaluate given Seed Packaging Methods for Extended Shelf Life of given seed sample.
  • Explore the role of automation in modern seed processing system along with its advantages and limitations. Provide examples of automated processes used in seed cleaning or packaging.
