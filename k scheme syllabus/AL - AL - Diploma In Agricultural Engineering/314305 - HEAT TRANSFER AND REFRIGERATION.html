I. RATIONALE

Agricultural engineers integrate the principles of heat transfer with refrigeration technology, providing students with a comprehensive understanding of thermal systems and their applications. Emphasis is placed on the theoretical foundations, practical design considerations, and real-world applications of heat transfer and refrigeration systems.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Develop, operate, and optimize thermal systems across a wide range of industries, including HVAC&R, aerospace, automotive, manufacturing, energy, and more.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify fundamental principles and mechanisms of heat transfer.
  • CO2 - Improve proficiency one-dimensional steady-state conduction analysis
  • CO3 - Analyze one-dimensional convective heat transfer.
  • CO4 - Analyze heat transfer mechanisms and design evaporator system components.
  • CO5 - Suggest fundamental principles of refrigeration systems.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • Assess the efficiency of fins and the effectiveness of thermal insulation in improving convective heat transfer. Discuss the significance of the findings and their implications for practical applications.(prepare presentation)
  • A case study to assess thermal contact resistance and determine the ideal insulation thickness for various one-dimensional conduction situations.
  • Prepare a Report on heat transfer methods (conduction, convection, and radiation) involved in the cooling process of a metal object in an industrial setting.(Submit online)
  • Prepare a Report on radiation heat transfer system considering emissive power, reflectivity, and transmissivity to meet specific performance metrics.
    • Micro project
  • Analyze the effect of cross-sectional area variation on one-dimensional steady-state conduction through cylinders and spheres: A comparative study.
  • Develop and optimize radiation heat transfer systems considering factors such as emissive power, reflectivity, and transmissivity to achieve desired performance metrics.
  • Explore methods to enhance convective heat transfer using fins and evaluate their effectiveness through empirical correlations and experimental analysis
  • Test various types of evaporators including natural circulation, forced circulation, and falling film evaporators to analyze their performance in terms of capacity, economy, and boiling point elevation.
  • Develop a vapor compression refrigeration system, analyze its key components including compressor, condenser, evaporator, and expansion valve
