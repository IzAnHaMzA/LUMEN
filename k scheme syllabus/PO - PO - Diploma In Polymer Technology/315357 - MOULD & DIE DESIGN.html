I. RATIONALE

This course empowers polymer technicians to design and draw various parts and systems for injection, blow, compression, and transfer molds. They can create feed, cooling, and ejection systems of multiple molds, which will help design, classify, and maintain molds for plastic industries. The course also gives orientation on the construction and working of dies used for Film, Sheet, Pipe, and Wire-cable dies. This course finds vast use in industrial applications and will be very useful for the placement of students.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Apply design principles to solve practical problems in mould and die design, such as improving efficiency, reducing defects, and optimizing performance.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Apply the knowledge of injection mould terminology to classify various moulds.
  • CO2 - Choose the appropriate feed, cooling and ejection system for the given injection moulded product.
  • CO3 - Use the knowledge of compression mould and transfer mould to select the appropriate type of mould for specific applications.
  • CO4 - Select appropriate blow mould designs for given applications.
  • CO5 - Apply die design concepts to select relevant dies for products like sheets, films, pipes, and cables.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Create 3D models of any of the flash type, semi-positive type, and positive type compression moulds using CAD software.
  • Prepare a chart of common faults observed and their remedies for compression mould, injection mould, transfer mould and blow mould.
  • Construct a 3D model of an injection mould, showing all the components such as the fixed half, moving half, core, cavity, plates, guide pin, sprue bush, etc. Use CAD software to create detailed diagrams and present your design in a report.
  • Create a detailed case study on a real-world injection moulding project that utilizes either a hot runner, runnerless, or split mould. Analyze the project's design choices, challenges faced, and outcomes. Present your case study in both written and presentation formats.
  • Conduct a case study on a real-world injection moulding project, focusing on the feed system. Analyze the design choices for the sprue, runner, and gate, and evaluate their impact on the overall efficiency and quality of the moulded parts. Prepare a report and present it to the class.
  • Investigate different types of cooling systems used in industry for various mould configurations. Compare their efficiency, cost, and applicability. Prepare a detailed report and presentation summarizing your findings and providing recommendations for best practices.
  • Design a heating system for a specific mould or die application using one of the heating methods. Create a detailed CAD drawing of the heating system and explain its design features and operation. Prepare a presentation to showcase the design and its advantages for the selected application.
  • Create a 3D models of any of the extrusion blow mould, injection blow mould and stretch blow mould using CAD software.
  • Create a 3D models of any of the pipe die, sheet die and film die using CAD software.
  • Prepare a chart of common faults observed and their remedies for compression mould, injection mould, transfer mould and blow mould.
  • Prepare a wooden/3D printed prototype/models of any of the type of injection moulds, compression moulds, transfer moulds and extrusion dies
    • Assignment
  • Write a detailed report on flash type, semi-positive type, and positive type compression moulds. Include constructional details and function of each component of the mould. Estimate the bill of material for each type of mould by surveying nearby industries.
  • Write a comprehensive report on integral pot, auxiliary ram, and loose plate transfer moulds. Include constructional details and function of each component of the mould. Estimate the bill of material for each type of mould by surveying nearby industries.
  • Analyse a real-world example where different types of sprue bushes are used in transfer moulds and discuss the impact of sprue bush design on the moulding process and product quality.
  • Write a detailed report on heating systems for moulds and dies. Include detail addresses of manufacturers, dealers and costing by surveying through nearby market
  • Write a report on blow moulds used for making any three popular blow moulded product found in market. Include constructional details and working of the blow moulds.
  • Write and present a report in a class on various types of dies used in pipe industry, sheet industry and film industry. Include constructional details and working of dies.
