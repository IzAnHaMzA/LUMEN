I. RATIONALE

The packaging industry is a significant global sector, and polymers represent a substantial portion of the materials used. Polymer Packaging is a crucial addition to the curriculum for a Diploma in polymer technology, particularly for those specializing in materials science related fields. Moreover, this course not only equips students with technical knowledge but also fosters a holistic view of the field, preparing them for future challenges and opportunities in the industry

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Students will be able to operate machines and equipment related to packaging
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Apply the selection criteria to choose suitable packaging materials for a given application
  • CO2 - Apply the knowledge of flexible packaging to choose the appropriate packaging for a given product
  • CO3 - Select the appropriate thermoforming method for a given application
  • CO4 - Use the appropriate packaging and decoration process for a given application
  • CO5 - Conduct the tests on different packaging materials and implement the controls for packaging hazards

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Micro project
  • Download videos of flexible packaging, rigid packaging, package conversion, decoration processes and show in front of the class
  • Prepare a chart of rigid packaging with examples and submit to department
  • Prepare a presentation by collecting information on government regulations and norms for polymer packaging material
  • Collect different polymer materials commonly used in packaging and prepare a technical report
  • Design a specialized flexible packaging solution for a food product of your choice using aseptic packaging, vacuum packaging or modified atmosphere packaging. Specify the materials, structure, properties and performance requirements of the packaging. Justify your design choices based on the characteristics and shelf life of the food product
  • Create a presentation or infographic comparing the different thermoforming methods and their applications
  • Visit a local packaging manufacturer and observe the extrusion blow moulding and injection blow moulding processes. Write a report summarizing the steps, equipment, materials, and quality parameters involved in each process
  • Design and create a specialized packaging solution for a product of your choice using one of the sealing methods. Specify the materials, structure, properties, and performance requirements of the packaging. Justify your design choices based on the characteristics of the product
  • Create a presentation or infographic comparing the different decoration processes and their applications
  • Conduct an experiment to measure the compatibility, gloss, product loss, stress crack resistance, migration, stack load, drop, and vibration of different packaging materials using appropriate instruments and methods
  • Visit a local packaging manufacturer and observe the skin, shrink, and blister packaging, stretch wrapping, pouching, and bag making processes. Write a report summarizing the steps, equipment, materials, and quality parameters involved in each process
  • Create a presentation explaining the different types of packaging hazards and their controls. Provide examples of how these hazards can be mitigated in the packaging industry
