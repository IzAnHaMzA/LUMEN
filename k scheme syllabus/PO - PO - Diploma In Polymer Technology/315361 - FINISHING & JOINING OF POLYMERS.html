I. RATIONALE

Many polymer products require some degree of machining and finishing after they have been processed and before they are used for their respective application. For functional purposes, industrial polymeric products must often be assembled with similar or dissimilar products. Parts made of polymers also need to be decorated, e.g., with printing, metallization, etc., for a better aesthetic appearance. The polymer diploma technologist can apply basic machining, finishing, joining, and decorating techniques used with polymers in the industries, which may help them become entrepreneurs.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use the proper finishing and decorative techniques to improve the polymer surface.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Use relevant finishing and machining techniques for polymer products for specific applications.
  • CO2 - Apply knowledge of joining methods to select appropriate adhesives for joining polymer.
  • CO3 - Select appropriate welding techniques used for joining polymeric products.
  • CO4 - Choose suitable decorating techniques for polymers used in aesthetic application.
  • CO5 - Apply relevant powder coating to improve surface characteristics.
