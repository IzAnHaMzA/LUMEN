I. RATIONALE

Mould manufacturing processes are the techniques used to create moulds, which are hollow containers that can shape molten or solid materials into desired forms. Moulds are widely used in various industries, such as plastics, metals, ceramics, glass, rubber, and food. Mould manufacturing processes involve designing, selecting, preparing, and assembling the mould components, as well as operating and maintaining the moulding machines and equipment. Polymer diploma engineers (also called technologists) have to deal with mould-making in the specialized areas of manufacturing. They need to work as a mould makers in the plastic industries. In mould-making, diploma polymer engineers have to work with conventional and modern machining tools like lathe, milling, slotting, grinding, lapping, buffing, super finishing, electro-discharge machines, CNC machines, etc. This course has been developed in a way that imparts skills and competencies for the selection of materials, manufacturing various mold components, their assembly, and heat treatment process.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
• Students should be able to fabricate simple moulds used in various plastic processing and moulding techniques using relevant fabrication methods and machines for specified tasks.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select suitable material for making different components of mould.
  • CO2 - Fabricate mould components by using various conventional machining techniques.
  • CO3 - Fabricate mould components by using modern machining techniques.
  • CO4 - Select suitable heat treatment and finishing method for a given mould component.
  • CO5 - Prepare assembly of given mould components.
