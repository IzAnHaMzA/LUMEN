I. RATIONALE

This course is crucial for textile diploma engineers as it focuses on essential processes namely warping and sizing which are fundamental to weaving preparatory techniques. Designed for diploma engineers, it empowers them to create woven fabrics by applying principles of warping and sizing, providing a foundational understanding of warp preparation and its application in resolving diverse textile weaving challenges.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Prepare the warpers/weavers beam using the warping and sizing process by adhering to the specifications required by the textile industry sector.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Use the beam warping machine to produce a warper beam with releavant process parameters.
  • CO2 - Use the sectional warping machine to produce the required warp pattern in the weaver’s beam.
  • CO3 - Prepare size paste for given yarn type and count.
  • CO4 - Produce the weaver’s beam by setting the relevant process parameters of the sizing machine.
  • CO5 - Use quality checking parameters to ensure the required quality of sized yarn.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Choose the warping process based on a given type of fabric.
TLO 1.2 Prepare a warper beam by adjusting the process parameters on the direct warping machine for given fabric parameters.
TLO 1.3 Calculate warping machine production in Kg/shift with given parameters.
 Unit - I Principles of Direct Warping
1.1 Objectives and significance of warping process, Types of warping, and importance of each: Beam warping, sectional warping, single-end warping, ball warping, Differentiate between various warping processes. Criteria of selection of sectional warping and beam warping process. Types of yarn packages: over-end withdrawal and side withdrawal.
1.2 Direct warping: Passage of yarn through the direct warping machine. Creels: Types, merits, and demerits of each, Tensioners: necessity and types.
1.3 Headstock components: Stop motion- its significance on the quality of the beam, Expanding comb and their types, Pressure drum, Doffing-manual and automatic, Defects in warping beams- uneven density, ridges, bulged or sunken selvages, nonuniform length.
1.4 Calculation: Production, Number of beams in a set, Number of ends in a beam, efficiency from given parameters.

 TLO 2.1 Describe the process sequence for production weavers beam of given stripe fabric on a sectional warping machine.
TLO 2.2 Prepare the weavers beam by adjusting the process parameters on the sectional warping machine for given fabric parameters.
TLO 2.3 Calculate the number of sections, section width, creel capacity, and number of ends on the beam, production, and efficiency in the section warping machine.
 Unit - II Principles of Sectional Warping
2.1 Sectional warping: objectives of sectional warping. Passage of yarn through the sectional warping machine. The process sequence of sectional warping involved in the production of color pattern
2.2 Machine components: Creel and its type, Function of separating rods, leasing reed. Construction of sectional drum and its traverse. Leasing process, Beaming process, sectional warping beam defects, and their causes
2.3 Calculate production, efficiency, number of sections, section width, creel capacity for a given process, and fabric parameters.

 TLO 3.1 Choose size paste ingredients for give yarn type and count.
TLO 3.2 Explain the procedure for preparation of size paste.
TLO 3.3 Explain the importance of size paste properties, keeping, congealing, viscosity, and concentration.
 Unit - III Preparation of Size Paste
3.1 Sizing ingredients and their role: adhesive, softeners, Antiseptics, Weighing agent, Wetting agent. Antistatic agent, Deliquescent agent, defoaming agent, etc. Traditional sizing components used in India process (IKS). Adhesives: Classification of adhesives, physical properties of starch, Factors governing formulation of size paste
3.2 Size paste preparation: mixer, pressure cooker, storage, and transport to the size box. Cooking of size paste in a pressure cooker, injection cooker.
3.3 Size paste properties and their significance: Keeping, congealing, viscosity, and concentration. Testing of adhesives: Microscope method, Fineness, Viscosity, Moisture content, Ash content, Cold soluble matter.

4
 TLO 4.1 Select the suitable creel for the given set of beams with justification.
TLO 4.2 Describe with a sketch the working of the unwinding zone, sizing zone, drying and splitting zone, and headstock for the given sort.
TLO 4.3 Choose the suitable process parameters of the saw box, drying cylinders, and headstock components for the given yarn with justification.
TLO 4.4 Explain the driving arrangement on the sizing machine to control the stretch.
 Unit - IV Sizing Machine and Its Components
4.1 Passage of yarn through two-cylinder and multi-cylinder sizing machine. Creel types: Horizontal creel or over and under creel, Vertical creel: top withdrawal and bottom withdrawal, Inclined creel: top withdrawal and bottom withdrawal, Equi-tension creel, Magazine creel, Merits and demerits of each. Leasing of warp. The braking system creels the number of warp beams required for given fabric parameters.
4.2 Size box, its components, and their role: conventional and modern size box, immersing roller, Squeezing roller, Temperature controlling devices- thermostat tube, Size paste control system, Electrical system. Wet splitting and its advantage.
4.3 Importance of Drying. type of drying: cylinder, hot air, radiative. Mechanism of drying in the cylinder. Factors affecting the rate of drying on cylinders. comparing 2-cylinder and multicylinder drying. Dry splitting arrangement
4.4 Function of headstock components: comb, Sheeting roller, Drag roller, Driving arrangement: Muti-motor drive, Reeves/PIV gear drive, Measuring and marking motion

 Unit - V Quality Aspects of Sizing Process
5.1 Define size pick up, Significance of size pick, Measurement of size pick up percentage, Factors affecting size pick up, concept of size add-on measurement of size add-on
5.2 Define stretch, Importance of stretch, Methods of measurement of stretch, control of stretch at The creel zone, sizing, drying and splitting zone, and winding zone. Define Migration and lappers, factors affecting the migration
5.3 Calculate dead loss, machine production, and efficiency of machine, number of ends on the weaver beam.
