I. RATIONALE

Fibrous materials have been used for centuries to produce yarn, fabric, and various textile structures. These materials can come from both natural and synthetic sources. Textile Manufacturing technologists must have knowledge of textile fibers. The source of fiber, the manufacturing process, and their physical and chemical properties. This course helps the technologies for the selection of relevant fiber used in specific applications and choosing fiber specific like dyeing, finishing, and wet processes.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
"Select the relevant fiber to develop textile articles."
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Identify relevant characteristics of textile fibers and polymers.
  • CO2 - Select the natural textile fiber based on physical and chemical properties.
  • CO3 - Choose relevant spinning methods for production of given man-made fibres
  • CO4 - Use Chemical process to synthesize regenerated fibers.
  • CO5 - Prepare synthetic fibers through appropriate manufacturing method.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Differentiate the essential and desirable properties of fiber used in textiles.
TLO 1.2 Interpret the properties of textile fibers based on crystalline and amorphous structure.
TLO 1.3 Define the terminologies polymer, degree of polymer and condensation polymerization reaction
TLO 1.4 Use the characteristics of fiber forming polymers to produce synthetic fibers.
 Unit - I Characteristics of Textile Fibers and Fiber Forming Polymers
1.1 Define- Textile fiber and yarn. classify the textile fibers. Essential and desirable properties of fibers used in textiles.
1.2 Fiber Structure - Define the crystalline and amorphous regions of textile fibers. Effect of crystalline and amorphous region on fiber's physical properties such as strength, elongation, stiffness, absorption, density, and melting point of the fiber.
1.3 Define- polymer, polymerization process and degree of polymerization. Types of chemical reactions during polymerizations- condensation and addition
1.4 Characteristics of fiber forming polymer- molecular weight, molecular chain orientation, nature of crystalline and amorphous region etc.

TLO 2.2 Explain the manufacturing process of Jute fiber.
TLO 2.3 Describe the physical, chemical properties and morphological structure of wool fiber
TLO 2.4 Explain the sericulture of silk.
 Unit - II Properties of Natural Fibers
2.1 Cotton fiber- Indian history of cotton cultivation and harvesting (IKS). Physical and chemical properties of cotton fiber, morphological structure and Cross-section, applications of cotton in Indian culture.
2.2 Jute fiber- Indian traditional Jute cultivation, harvesting (IKS), retting, and Extraction. Physical and chemical properties of Jute fiber, Applications of jute fiber in Indian culture.
2.3 Wool fiber- Indian wool breeds and traditional shearing methods (IKS), Physical and chemical properties of Jute fiber. Morphological structure and Cross-section, Applications of wool fiber in Indian culture.
2.4 Silk fiber- chemical nature. Silk varieties - Wild & domesticated. Life cycle of silk. Indian Sericulture (IKS). Physical and chemical properties of silk fiber. morphological structure and Cross-section. Applications of silk fiber in Indian culture.
 Presentations
Video Demonstrations
3
 TLO 3.1 Describe with neat sketch the melt spinning process in detail
TLO 3.2 Describe with neat sketch the wet spinning process in detail
TLO 3.3 Compare dry and melt spinning process.
TLO 3.4 Describe the manufacturing method of LOY(Low Oriented Yarn), MOY (Medium Oriented Yarn), POY (Partially Oriented Yarn), HOY (High Oriented Yarn)and FDY (Fully Drawn Yarn) types of yarn.
 Unit - III Fiber Spinning Methods
3.1 Introduction to melt, wet and dry spinning methods. Concept of thermoplastic and thermoset fibers. Advantages and disadvantages of synthetic fibers.
3.2 Melt spinning fiber manufacturing process-Melting, extrusion, solidification (one way heat transfer), drawing and winding. Advantages and Disadvantages. examples of fibers manufactured
3.3 Wet spinning fiber manufacturing process- Dope preparation, extrusion, solidification (Inside coagulation bath/mass transfer), washing, drawing and winding. Advantages and Disadvantages. Examples of fibers manufactured
3.4 Dry spinning fiber manufacturing process- Dope preparation, extrusion, solidification (one way heat and one way mass transfer), drawing and winding. Advantages and Disadvantages. examples of fibers manufactured
3.5 Types of yarn- LOY (Low Oriented Yarn), POY (Partially Oriented Yarn), HOY (High Oriented Yarn), FDY (Fully Drawn Yarn) type of yarns. Differentiate between types of yarns based on fiber orientation, draw ratio, shelf life, winding speed, presence of crystalline and amorphous regions.
