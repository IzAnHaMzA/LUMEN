I. RATIONALE

In textile industry optimizing process parameters are essential for ensuring both efficiency and product quality. Students need to analyze the impact of these parameters and effectively interpret process-related data to achieve the required production, quality and manufacturing cost of textile product.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Manage textile department process parameters to produce high-quality yarn and fabric at the lowest possible cost.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Select relevant key variables to control process in blow room and carding to produce desired quality of sliver.
  • CO2 - Set standard norms for relevant process variable to control draw-frame, comber preparatory and comber processes to produce even sliver.
  • CO3 - Use the relevant process control methodologies on speed frame and ring frame machines to achieve desired yarn quality.
  • CO4 - Control the weaving preparatory process parameters to achieve the required quality of beam.
  • CO5 - Produce woven fabric with the required quality, and design specification

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Explain the scope for process control in spinning.
TLO 1.2 Use linear programming technique to optimise of cotton mixing quality
TLO 1.3 Use HVI equipment for bale management.
TLO 1.4 Measure trash content in cotton using trash analyser.
 Unit - I Mixing ,Blow room and Carding
1.1 Process control in Mixing: Scope of process control in spinning, Key variables for process control, significance of process control in mixing.
1.2 Control of mixing quality through fibre characteristics, simultaneous control of mixing cost and quality, Linear programming technique in optimization of cotton mixing.
1.3 Bale management using HVI, cotton property evaluation using HVI and AFIS.
1.4 Process control in Blow Room and Carding: Determination of trash content and cleaning efficiency and their norms, Measurement of lint losses, fibre rupture, and neps, their norms and its control.
1.5 Lap uniformity and its norms, batt control in chute feed, Norms and control of sliver uniformity (CV% and U%).
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Presentations
Site/Industry Visit
2
 TLO 2.1 Correlate the influence of machine speed, settings on draw frame sliver quality.
TLO 2.2 Judge the combing performance based on quality of lap.
TLO 2.3 Determine the noil percentage, and nep removal efficiency in comber.
TLO 2.4 Calculate fractionating efficiency of comber.
 Unit - II Draw-frame, Comber preparatory, and Comber
2.1 Process control in Draw frame: Significance of the process control, Norms and control of irregularities in sliver, drafting wave & its significance, influence of parameters like speed, settings on sliver quality, Role of auto leveler.
2.2 Process control in comber preparatory: Significance and importance of good lap for comber, influence of feed stock on combing performance.
2.3 Process control in comber: Significance of the process control, Factors Influencing the combing performance as machine components and settings, Method of measurement of noil %, nep removal efficiency its control and norms.
2.4 Fractionating efficiency of comber, Norms and control of combed sliver uniformity.
 Lecture Using Chalk-Board
Presentations
Flipped Classroom
3
 TLO 3.1 Correlate the influence of various process parameters on roving quality.
TLO 3.2 Establish the relation between various machine and material parameters on yarn quality.
TLO 3.3 Control yarn evenness and imperfections in ring frame.
TLO 3.4 Suggest steps to control yarn realisation of various yarn counts.
 Unit - III Speed Frame and Ring Frame
3.1 Process control in Speed Frame: Significance of process control, influence of process parameters like flyer speed, twist, break draft and settings on roving quality, Stretch control at speed frame, Norms and control of roving U% and count CV%.
3.2 Process control in Ring Frame: Significance of process control, Influence of various machine and material parameters on yarn quality.
3.3 Control of yarn count and strength its variability, within and between bobbin variation, Control of yarn evenness and imperfections. Types of yarn irregularities, measurement causes and assessment. Control of yarn hairiness.
3.4 Yarn realisation: Importance and factors affecting yarn realization, Estimation process, norms for various yarns like cotton, blended etc.
 Video Demonstrations
Presentations
4
 TLO 4.1 Select the relevant process control parameters to improve the productivity and efficiency of the winding machine.
TLO 4.2 Identify the relationship between key parameters of the warping process with respect to its quality of beam
TLO 4.3 Choose the relevant ingredients to prepare size paste for the given type and count of yarn.
TLO 4.4 Describe methods to control stretch, size pick-up and temperature in the given sizing machine.
 Unit - IV Weaving Preparatory
4.1 Types of yarn tensioners, nature of Unwinding tension. Causes and Remedies of package defects (Jali, patterning, soft base, corny flower).
4.2 Type of yarn clearer-mechanical and electronic, Uster classimate chart-5, Performance Assessment of winding machine – knot factor, clearing efficiency, retained splice strength.
4.3 Scope and approach of process control in Direct/Sectional Warping, Factor affecting the end breakages, factors governing the quality of beam
4.4 Causes of low production in warping, calculate production and efficiency of Direct/Sectional Warping process
4.5 Scope and approach of process control in sizing. Choice of size recipe and size pickup. testing of size paste- solid content, viscosity. care while preparing size paste.
4.6 Factors affecting the size pick up. control measure for stretch. Determine dead loss, calculate production and efficiency of sizing machine.
 Video Demonstrations
Flipped Classroom
Presentations
5
 TLO 5.1 Identify and control the given causes of warp and weft breakages in loom shed.
TLO 5.2 Assess the loom performance by evaluating efficiency for the given loom shed.
TLO 5.3 Optimise loom allocation for maximize operative efficiency and reduction in cost of production.
TLO 5.4 Control the hardwaste and set the norms for the same.
 Unit - V Loom-shed
5.1 Scope and approach of process control in loom shed. Factors affecting loom efficiency, warp stoppages, weft stoppages, and mechanical failure.
5.2 Methods to assess loom performance: snap technique, quality rounds, loom data, and centralised control.
5.3 Calculations: Optimum loom allocation, maximizing operative efficiency, the cost of production.
5.4 Hard waste: in weaving preparatory and loom shed, set the norms for the same. Fabric realization, Factors that decide the fabric realization.
5.5 Significance of fabric defect, causes and remedies of fabric defects. 4 and 10 point fabric grading system. Traditional fabric inspection systems(IKS)
Case Study
Presentations
