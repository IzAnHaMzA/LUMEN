I. RATIONALE

Textile manufacturing students will gain comprehensive knowledge of weaving principles and machinery mechanisms. The focus is on understanding fabric formation concepts, weaving machine mechanisms, identifying fabric faults, and developing fabrics with specific properties.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Weave the woven fabric with the specifications required for the textile industrial sector.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Prepare the "Pirn" using a weft preparatory machine with the required specifications.
  • CO2 - Set the mechanisms under the primary motions of the weaving loom according to the given fabric sort.
  • CO3 - Adjust the let-off and takeup mechanisms to produce fabric with the required pick density.
  • CO4 - Maintain auxiliary motion for weaving defect-free fabric.
  • CO5 - Identify various fabric defects and analyse the causes and remedies of the same.

 TLO 2.1 Determine the heald and reed count of given types of healds and reeds.

TLO 2.3 Describe the given types of shedding, picking, and beat-up mechanisms.
TLO 2.4 Use relevant timings and settings of shedding, picking, and beat-up mechanisms to produce the required specification of fabric.
 Unit - II Primary Motions of Weaving Machine.
2.1 Brief history of loom, Indian hand looms (IKS) Objectives of drawing-in and tying process with their importance. Types of healds and reeds. Estimate the head count and reed count in various systems.
2.2 Passage of yarn through weaving machine componenets. Objectives of primary, secondary, and auxiliary motions. Shedding mechanism - Introduction to tappet, dobby, jacquard, negative and positive shedding mechanism. construction and working of negative and postive tappet shedding mechanism. Early and late shedding with their significance.
2.3 Object and types of picking mechanisms. Construction and working of the cone over the pick and side lever under the pick mechanism. Ways to adjust picking force. Ealy and late picking timing with its significance. functions of parts involved in the shuttle box
2.4 Objects and types of beat-up mechanisms. Construction and working of crank beat up. Timing of beat-up.
 Presentations
Video Demonstrations
Site/Industry Visit

 Unit - III Secondary Motions of Weaving machine
3.1 Types of let-off mechanism- Negative, positive and semi-positive. advantages and disadvantage of each type. Construction and working of negative let-off mechanism. calculate the output warp tension. construction and working of oscillating back rest.
3.2 Types of take up mechanism-Negative, postitive, Intermittent (5-wheel & 7-wheel), and continuous. Differentiate between each type. Construction and working of 7-wheel intermittent take up mechansims. Calculation of divident and Pick wheel.
3.3 Gearing Diagram of plain power loom, Production of loom in meter/shift, kg/shift. calculations of warp weight, weft weight and GSM.

 Unit - IV Auxiliary Motions of Weaving Machine.
4.1 Objective of stop motion and its significance on weaving machine. Construction and working of Warp stop motion -Mechanical and Electrical warp stop motion. Weft Stop Motion – Types, construction and working of Side weft fork motion, Center weft fork Their merits and demerits.
4.2 Object of warp protection motion and its significance. Construction and working of loose reed and fast reed mechanism. comparison between them.
4.3 Temple Motion – Two roller, three roller, full width, temple bracket mounting, various types of temple rollers.

5.1 Brief description, demonstration, causes and remedies for warp way defects- double end, broken end, missing end, streaks, warp floats, stitches, wrong drawing-in,

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Self learning
  • Gather information on possible defects in the woven fabric and their relative proportion from the industry.
  • Explore the timing cycle of primary motions on the automatic loom from various resources.
    • Seminar
  • Positive let-off and take-up mechanism
  • Types of shuttles used in power and autoloom
    • Micro project
  • Conduct an experimental investigation to demonstrate the combined effect of varying the number of teeth on standard and change wheels in the take-up mechanism on picks spacing in fabric and present the findings
  • Establish the relation between the picking force and speed of the shuttle in the power loom and present the findings in terms of graphs and correlation equations
  • Design the negative shedding cam to produce the required weave and demonstrate its working through presentation/ working model
    • Assignment
  • Collect and compile the defect samples frequently observed in woven fabric during manufacturing. Name each and report possible causes and remedies.
  • Inspect power looms of diverse widths within the weaving industry and document their respective sley eccentricities.
  • Report the current practice in the power loom industry to maintain uniform tension on warp from full to empty beam.
