I. RATIONALE

A comprehensive understanding of rotor spinning, air-jet spinning, friction spinning, blend spinning, texturising and yarn doubling is imperative for textile technologists aiming to produce various spun and filament yarns. These processes are pivotal in the manufacturing of rotor-spun yarn, air-jet spun yarn, friction-spun yarn, blended yarn, double yarn, and textured yarn of desired quality. This course provides essential knowledge and skills for the production of various types of yarns that will be competitive in the market.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
Use the principles of open-end, false-twist, fibre blending, texturizing and yarn doubling to produce various types of yarn in the spinning industry.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Use Rotor spinning machine to produce coarse yarns.
  • CO2 - Use Air-jet and Friction spinning machines to produce technical yarns.
  • CO3 - Apply principle of blending to produce blend yarns.
  • CO4 - Use yarn doubling machine to produce double yarns.
  • CO5 - Produce textured yarns using relevant texturising machine.

V. THEORY LEARNING OUTCOMES AND ALIGNED COURSE CONTENT
Sr.No Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's)aligned to CO's. Learning content mapped with Theory Learning Outcomes (TLO's) and CO's. Suggested Learning Pedagogies.
1
 TLO 1.1 Explain principle of open-end spinning process.
TLO 1.2 Trace the passage of material through rotor spinning machine with neat sketch.
TLO 1.3 Demonstrate the different zones of rotor spinning machine.
TLO 1.4 State the properties of rotor spun yarn.
TLO 1.5 Summaries the modern developments in rotor spinning.
 Unit - I Rotor Spinning
1.1 Introduction: Limitations of ring spinning system, Classification of unconventional spinning systems. Principle of open-end spinning process.
1.2 Rotor spinning: Raw material requirement and preparation of sliver for rotor spinning, Construction and working of rotor spinning.
1.3 Operating Regions: Sliver Feed, Opening unit, Fiber transport to the rotor, Back doubling, Wrapper Fibres, Rotor, Rotor speed and diameter, Rotor cleaning, Rotor bearing and Drive, yarn formation and twist insertion, yarn take-off, yarn winding.
1.4 Influence of opening roller speed, rotor diameter, rotor speed on yarn quality.
1.5 Yarn structure and properties of rotor spun yarns, application of rotor spun yarn
1.6 Modern features of rotor spinning machine, Production calculations.

TLO 2.4 Describe the passage of material through DREF-2 friction spinning machine with neat sketch.
TLO 2.5 Analyze the structure of friction spun yarn with neat sketch.
 Unit - II Air-jet and Friction Spinning
2.1 Air-jet Spinning: Raw material requirement, Principle of Air jet spinning, Construction and working of air jet spinning machines (Jet Spinner and Vortex Spinner),
2.2 Influence of process parameters on properties of air jet spun yarns.
2.3 Yarn structure and properties of air-jet spun yarn, end use applications of air-jet spun yarn. Modern features of Air-jet Spinning.
2.4 Friction Spinning: Raw material used, Principle of friction spinning, Construction and working of DREF-2 and DREF-3 spinning.
2.5 Yarn formation on friction-spinning machines: Fibre feeding device, Fibre transport device, Twisting device.
2.6 Yarn structure and properties of friction spun yarn, end use applications of friction spun yarn. Modern features of friction spinning.

TLO 3.3 Explain in detail Blow Room blending.

 Unit - III Blend Spinning
3.1 Introduction, Objectives of blending, Indices of blending (Degree of mixing and Index of blend irregularity).
3.2 Pre-blending processes: Selection of blend constituents, Spin finish application, acclimatization of fibres, fibre tinting.
3.3 Methods of blending: Blow Room, Lap blending, Fleece blending, Sliver blending, Web blending, Traditional blending method (IKS)
3.4 Changes to be made at each cotton spinning machines for processing of manmade fibres and thier blends.
3.5 Common faults in blended yarn.

TLO 4.4 Use the principle of formation of yarn core-spun yarn.
 Unit - IV Yarn Doubling and Fancy yarns
4.1 Yarn Doubling: Introduction, Objects of yarn doubling, types of double yarn.
4.2 Yarn Doubling Machines: Construction and working of conventional ring doubling machine, Preparatory process for two for one twister (Parallel Winding), Design and construction of two for one twisting machine.
4.3 Calculation of twist, twist contraction, resultant count of double yarns, production calculation of two for one twister.
4.4 Fancy yarns: basic principle, Classification of fancy yarns such as Spiral, Gimp, Loop, Snarl, Knop, Cover, Slub, Neppy, Multi count, Multi-twist, Manufacturing of slub, multi-count, Siro yarn, and multi-twist yarn.
4.5 Specialty yarns: Principles of formation of yarn such as Core-spun yarn and Melange yarn, Process and sequence used for production of Mélange yarn.

TLO 5.4 Adjust process parameters for required texturizing effect.
 Unit - V Texturising
5.1 Texturizing: Objectives, Classification of textured yarn- stretched, modified, bulk yarn. Advantages of textured yarn. Methods of texturizing- false twist, gear crimping, stuffer box, edge crimping, knit-de-knit, air jet.
5.2 Principle of false twist texturizing, construction and working of draw texturizing machine, Modern features of draw texturising machine.
5.3 Principle of air-jet texturizing, construction and working of air jet texturizing machine, Modern features of Air-jet texturising machine.
5.4 Factors influencing texturizing process- Time, Temperature, Twist, and Tension.
