I. RATIONALE

Biomaterials play an important role in medical applications to support, enhance or replace damaged tissue or a biological function. Important properties of biomaterials include biocompatibility, mechanical utility and resistance to corrosion and wear. This course is intended to understand how materials interact with human body and select appropriate engineering materials to serve physiological functions in the healthcare industry.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to attain the following industry/employer expected outcome through various teaching learning experiences:
Select biomaterials for implants used in medical applications.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Justify the biocompatibility of biomaterials with the living system.
  • CO2 - Choose the appropriate biomaterials to be used for dental and orthopedic applications.
  • CO3 - Identify the ceramics and polymers to be used for different medical applications.
  • CO4 - Identify the appropriate biomaterials to be used for cardiovascular and ophthalmic implants.
  • CO5 - Select appropriate biomaterials on the basis of testing before implantation in the human body.
