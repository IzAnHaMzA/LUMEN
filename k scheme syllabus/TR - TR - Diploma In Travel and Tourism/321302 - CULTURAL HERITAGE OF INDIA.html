I. RATIONALE

India is one of the ancient civilizations of the world with immense cultural heritage. Cultural Heritage is a concept which offers a bridge between the past and the future. Our heritage serves as the identity of our nation. Indian art, architecture, music, language, philosophy and religion reflect the diversity of influence that has occurred through centuries on our country and hence, India has something to offer to the tourists. India, with its vast history and cultural diversity, is home to numerous ancient sites and heritage monuments that are of immense significance. The people in India celebrate different festivals as they belong to different religions and follow diverse customs and traditions. Being a land of multiple cultures, religions, ethics, languages, handloom and handicrafts, India is a major tourist destination for both domestic and foreign tourists. The course will highlight the cultural tourism products of India ranging from places of religious importance, monuments in different architectural styles, different fairs and festivals celebrated, types of handlooms and handicrafts, Classical and Folk dances and museums of India. This course will make the students aware of the tourism potential of the cultural heritage that India is endowed with.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to help the student to attain the following industry-identified outcome through various teaching-learning experiences: Apply Indian traditions and heritage knowledge- information for planning, designing, and selling tourism products and services.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Interpret heterogeneity and principles of different religions for tourism.
  • CO2 - Identify Indian traditions through different types of architecture.
  • CO3 - Use the fairs and festivals celebrated in India for planning tours.
  • CO4 - Deploy the major handloom and handicrafts of India for souvenirs.
  • CO5 - Integrate the various classical and folk dances of India in tour packages.
  • CO6 - Employ the major museums of India in designing tour packages.
