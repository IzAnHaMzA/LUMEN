I. RATIONALE

Garmenting involves the transformation of fabric into a finished garment through a series of processes. The chemical treatments designed to enhance the fabric and garment's value. Certain garments derive their functional properties from specific chemical processing techniques. Therefore, a fundamental understanding of chemical processing is crucial for diploma engineers working in the field. These engineers are anticipated to apply their knowledge of chemical processing to impart the necessary properties to a given garment.

II. INDUSTRY / EMPLOYER EXPECTED OUTCOME
The aim of this course is to help the student to attain the following industry identified competency through various teaching learning experiences: Apply relevant chemical processing methods for various properties in fabric and garment.
III. COURSE LEVEL LEARNING OUTCOMES (COS)
Students will be able to achieve & demonstrate the following COs on completion of course based learning
  • CO1 - Apply relevant mechanical/chemical pre-treatment to remove the faults from grey fabrics.
  • CO2 - Use relevant dyes and chemicals for colouration of natural fibres.
  • CO3 - Use relevant dyes and chemicals for colouration of synthetic fibres.
  • CO4 - Use relevant printing method for the various fabrics/garments.
  • CO5 - Apply application based special printing effects for garments.

VII. SUGGESTED MICRO PROJECT / ASSIGNMENT/ ACTIVITIES FOR SPECIFIC LEARNING / SKILLS DEVELOPMENT (SELF LEARNING)
Assignment
  • List the acids used in various textile pre-treatments. Write the role of chemicals in a particular process. State its properties. Suggest alternative chemicals.
  • List the alkali used in various textile pre-treatments. Write the role of chemicals in a particular process. State its properties. Suggest alternative chemicals.
    • Micro project
  • Visit a local Industry. List the chemical used in pre-treatment process. Find out information of that chemical such as strength, manufacturing process, role of chemical, market cost. (Data collection)
  • List the dyes used in dyeing and printing. Find out information of that chemical such as manufacturing process, role of that dye, market cost.(Data collection)
  • Visit a local Industry. Collect the sample from grey to mercerization and observe the changes take places in each step. (Sample collection)
  • Produce the different shade by using same colour and write the observation. Batch of students should prepare relative process parameter chart. (Shade checking)
  • Collect the photographs of garment in which special printing effects are used. Classify them and write a brief about each category. (Garment collection)
  • Prepare chart on chemicals used in textile preparatory processes with their pH. Choose combinations of various chemicals preparatory processes and check their effect and summarize it in the form of chart. (pH in wet processing)
